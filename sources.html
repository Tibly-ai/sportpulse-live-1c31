How a headline reaches you

From a reporter's post to your screen, usually in under ninety seconds. Here's the whole chain, no black box.

01

We watch the wire

Our system tracks verified accounts from national networks, league press offices, and credentialed beat writers around the clock, flagging anything posted in the last sixty seconds.

02

A human checks it

Before anything hits the ticker, an editor cross-references it against a second independent source. Single-source rumors get held until they're confirmed.

03

It hits the feed

Confirmed updates push straight to Live Scores and Top Headlines, timestamped and credited back to the original source account.

The Roster

Six kinds of sources, one feed

We don't rely on any single outlet. Coverage gets stronger when we stack these together.

01

National Networks

ESPN, Fox Sports, CBS Sports, and NBC Sports feeds for league-wide scores and top-level breaking news.

02

League Wire Services

Official NFL, NBA, MLB, NHL, and MLS communications offices for rulings, injury designations, and transactions.

03

Team Beat Writers

The reporters who sit in the locker room every day, credentialed and covering a single team for a living.

04

Local Affiliates

Regional TV and radio sports desks that catch stories the national outlets are slower to pick up.

05

Stat and Odds Desks

Data providers that keep our live scoreboard synced to the second, box scores included.

06

Player-Direct Accounts

Verified athlete and coach accounts, used only to confirm or clarify what's already reported elsewhere.
